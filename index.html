This tool can help you decide whether to attend an in-person event that includes people you don't live with.

This is not medical advice! If you are unclear on your risk, or have a pre-existing condition that makes you especially vulnerable to COVID-19, talk to your doctor.

1. Where is the event?

2. Is the event indoors or outdoors?

3. How big is the space?

4. How many people will attend, including you?

5. How many people will be wearing a mask?

6. How long will you stay?

7. Will you take public transportation to get there?

8. Will you be using shared/public restrooms?

9. Will people at the event be drinking alcohol?

RESULT

Do not go unless you absolutely have to. To make the event safer: Go, but remain cautious. To make the event safer:
  • Move it outdoors
  • Move it to a larger venue
  • Include fewer people
  • Encourage more mask-wearing
  • Shorten your stay
  • Use a non-public form of transportation
  • Plan ahead to avoid using public/shared restrooms
  • Encourage less alcohol consumption
No matter what, there will be some amount of risk of coronavirus infection with any public activity. Always:
  • Keep your distance from others – at least 6 feet outdoors, 10 feet indoors
  • Wash your hands frequently.
  • Wear a mask.
  • Stay home as much as possible, preferably leaving only for essential tasks.
  • Follow the mandates, laws, and advice of your location's government and health officials.
